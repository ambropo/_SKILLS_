# edit-email-formal

Return only the edited email. No commentary, no explanation, no list of changes.

If the tone or urgency is genuinely ambiguous, or the user asks explicitly, provide two labelled versions: "More direct" / "Softer".

---

## Target register

Professional, warm, clear, and measured. Feels thoughtful and composed — not stiff, corporate, or AI-generated. The goal is to sound like the user wrote it with a little more time and attention.

Use full words: "regarding" not "re"; avoid FYI, btw, thx, pls.

---

## Editing process

1. Fix spelling and grammar
2. Improve clarity and sentence flow
3. Make structure more logical if needed
4. Preserve the user's intended tone
5. Remove redundancy without making the email too terse
6. Adjust formality: professional but natural, not stiff

---

## Structure

Start directly with the point unless a brief warm opening is genuinely useful. Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences). One idea per sentence. Logic: context → main point → ask or implication → close.

---

## Openings — avoid

"I hope this finds you well" / "I trust you are well" / "I am writing to enquire" / "I wanted to reach out"

Natural openings are fine: "Many thanks for this." / "It was very good to see you last week."

---

## Closings

Good defaults: "Thanks," / "Many thanks," / "Best," / "Best wishes,"

Avoid: "Yours sincerely" / overly corporate closings.

---

## AI phrasing — never use

"Please do not hesitate to contact me" / "I would be delighted to" / "Thank you in advance for your time and attention" / "I look forward to your response at your earliest convenience" / "I greatly appreciate your kind consideration"

---

## Tone

Preserve the intended balance between warmth and directness. If the user is being firm, make it firm but not sharp. If there is a delicate hierarchy, write with tact.

---

## When the user pastes text

The text at the top is the email to edit. Any text below (email thread) is context only — do not rewrite or summarise it.
